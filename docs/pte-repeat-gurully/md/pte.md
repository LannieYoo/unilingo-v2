For Australian General & Skilled Migration (subclass 189 and 190 visas), a minimum PTE score of 65 is required in each of the four communicative skills, equivalent to an IELTS 6.5. Competent English demands a PTE score of 79+ and also attracts additional scores in the points-tested migration system. Many occupational streams need higher scores (79+).
 What PTE score is required for Canada PR? 

PTE Core is the accepted PTE exam for Canada permanent residency via Express Entry. The benchmark for the Federal Skilled Worker Program is a CLB 9, which is approximately the same to a PTE Core score of 88. Most Provincial Nominee Programs require a CLB 7 minimum, which is around 68 on PTE Core in each communicative skill. Though the requirements might change so it is advised to check the official website for confirmation.
 What is the difference between PTE Academic and PTE Core? 

PTE Academic is accepted for university admissions and skilled migration in Australia, the United Kingdom, New Zealand, and the United States, while PTE Core is designed for Canadian PR and work permit applications approved by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada. Both exams test Speaking, Writing, Reading, and Listening, but the PTE Core focuses more on real-world workplace communication.

Yes, PTE is accepted in Canada and Germany. In Canada, PTE Core is accepted for Canadian PR and work permit pathways including Express Entry, Provincial Nominee Programs, and the Canadian Experience Class. For study purpose in Canada and Germany PTE Academic is accepted.
