## Understanding Scoring of PTE Exam Pattern

The PTE scoring system evaluates English proficiency through two main skill categories, ensuring a thorough assessment.

### 1. Communicative Skills

This category evaluates your Speaking, Writing, Reading, and Listening performance. Each task in the test is directly tied to one or more of these areas. For example

- Speaking tasks assess how well you can express yourself verbally.
- Writing tasks evaluate your ability to construct clear and coherent written responses.
- Reading tasks test your understanding of written content.
- Listening tasks measure how accurately you can grasp and interpret spoken information.

Your scores in these areas are essential as they reflect how effectively you can communicate in real-world academic and professional settings.

### 2. Enabling Skills

This category works behind the scenes and evaluates the foundational elements of your language skills. These include:

**Content: **

Refers to how well your response addresses the question or task requirements.

**Evaluation:**

- The response must directly and comprehensively address the given topic or prompt for speaking and writing.
- Irrelevant or incomplete responses result in lower scores.

**Oral Fluency:**

Measures the natural flow and rhythm of your speech.

**Evaluation:**

- Speech should be smooth, uninterrupted, and free of unnatural pauses.
- Speaking too slowly, hesitating, or repeating words lowers the score.
- Tools assess consistency in tempo and delivery, not just the correctness of ideas.

**Pronunciation:**

Evaluate the clarity and intelligibility of your speech to native and non-native English speakers.

**Evaluation:**

- Your pronunciation must align with standard English sounds.
- Words should be clear and understandable without requiring excessive effort from the listener.
- Mispronunciations or heavy accents can negatively impact this score.

**Form:**

Relates to the structural requirements of the response.

**Evaluation:**

- For writing, sticking to word limits (e.g., 200-300 words for essays) and adhering to task-specific formats.
- For speaking, provide an answer of appropriate length

**Development, Structure, and Coherence:**

Assesses how logically and effectively you organize and present your ideas.

**Evaluation:**

- Your response must have a clear introduction, body, and conclusion in writing tasks.
- Ideas should flow logically, with proper use of linking phrases (e.g., "therefore," "however").
- Disjointed ideas or lack of focus affect this score.

**Grammar:**

Assess the grammatical accuracy and variety in your response.

**Evaluation:**

- Responses should use correct sentence structures, verb tenses, subject-verb agreement, and punctuation.
- A higher score is awarded for demonstrating a range of grammatical constructs (e.g., conditional sentences, passive voice).

**Vocabulary:**

Evaluates the range and appropriateness of your word choices.

**Evaluation:**

- Using precise, varied, and contextually appropriate vocabulary boosts your score.
- Avoiding repetitive or overly simple words is crucial.
- Misuse of words can detract from the clarity and quality of your response.

**Spelling:**

Ensures that written responses adhere to standard spelling conventions.

**Evaluation:**

- Responses are scored based on correct spelling in either British or American English.
- Frequent spelling errors lower the score, even if other aspects of the response are strong.

**General Linguistic Range:**

Assesses your ability to express complex ideas effectively.

**Evaluation:**

- Responses demonstrating a wide range of expressions, phrases, and sentence complexity score higher.
- Avoiding repetitive structures and showing the ability to adapt language for different contexts is key.

These enabling skills significantly impact your communicative skills performance. Improving these areas can positively affect multiple sections of the test. Your scores in these areas are essential as they reflect how effectively you can communicate in real-world academic and professional settings.

- Advanced AI technology evaluates integrated tasks to assign accurate scores for each skill, eliminating bias.

## How PTE Scores are calculated

Understand how scores are calculated

### Automated scoring system.

The PTE test is computer-based and scored using a machine learning system, but human experts play a key role in training and refining the scoring process. These experts evaluate test-taker responses for speaking and writing tasks, setting the automated system's standards.

### Human scorers are also involved in two specific situations:

- **Unusual Responses:** Human reviewers handle anomalies—responses that the machine cannot score reliably.
- **Certain Task Types:** Tasks like "Describe Image" and "Retell Lecture" always require human review to ensure content accuracy before finalizing scores.

### Scoring Breakdown:

- **Correct/Incorrect Tasks:** Some tasks are scored simply as correct (1 point) or incorrect (0 points).
- **Partial Credit Tasks:** Other tasks allow for partial credit. Fully correct responses earn maximum points, partly correct responses earn some points, and incorrect responses earn none.

For speaking and writing tasks, additional criteria like Content (relevance to the prompt) and Form (e.g., word count) are also scored. These factors contribute to overall and communicative skill scores. Examples of responses that score zero include:

- Writing off-topic (Content).
- Writing fewer than 120 words (Form).
- Using excessive pre-prepared or memorized material (Content).
- Use of AI for unbiased assessment.
- Weightage of different tasks.

- **B1 Level (PTE Score 43-58)** is typically required for undergraduate study. At this level, the candidate can interact in everyday situations but might struggle with more complex language tasks.
- **A2 Level (PTE Score 30-42)** is more basic and may be suited for foundation courses, where candidates can handle simple conversations and familiar topics.
- **The A1 Level (PTE Score 10-29)** represents a very basic level of proficiency, with the ability to handle simple, everyday interactions, but it is unsuitable for academic study.

### Receiving Your Results:

- PTE Results are typically provided within five business days of your test date.
- After your test, you'll receive an email notification when your **Score Report** is ready to download.
- The PTE Score Report is available in PDF format, but institutions must verify it electronically to be accepted. Printed copies are not verified or authorized.

- If it has been more than five working days, check your spam or junk folder for the email. If it's not there and you haven't been contacted, follow up with PTE support.

## Things To Remember

Make a note of this information for future reference.

### PTE Exam Validity: How Long Are Your PTE Scores Valid?

PTE scores don't last forever. They are valid for two years from the test date. You have up to two years to use your scores for university applications, visa purposes, or job opportunities. After that, your scores expire, and you must retake the exam.

### What Happens After Two Years?

Once your scores reach the two-year mark, they are no longer considered valid. This means that if you're still in the process of applying to universities or immigration authorities, you'll need to retake the exam to provide current results.

It's a good idea to track when you took the test, especially if you're working on long-term plans like studying abroad or applying for residency.

For instance, if you plan to apply for a visa, you must ensure your scores are within the validity period. A good way to avoid issues is to take the test well in advance and check the validity date to ensure your scores are accepted.

### Process For Requesting Rescore or Technical Review:

**Requesting a Machine Rescore**

You can request a machine rescore if you think your score is incorrect. Here's what you need to know:

- PTE tests are machine-scored, so score changes are rare.
- Only spoken and open-ended written responses will be rescored.
- If your score changes, it can go up or down.
- If your score changes, you will get a refund for the rescore fee ($125) but not the test fee.
- The new score will replace your old one, and a new score report will be issued.
- You can request a rescore only once per test.
- You must request a rescore within 14 days of receiving your score.
- A rescore is not allowed if you have already booked another test.
- To request, call the Customer Support team and pay over the phone.

**Requesting a Technical Review**

You can request a technical review if you think a technical issue affected your speaking score. Here's what you should know:

- A technical review checks for audio recording issues but does not change your score.
- The fee for a technical review is $50 (non-refundable if no issues are found).
- If issues are confirmed, your test and review fees will be refunded.
- If no issues are found, you will not get any refund, but you can book a new test at your cost.
- The result of the review will be shared within 15 business days.
- Apply using the Customer Support form within 15 days of receiving your score.
- Both services are optional and depend on your specific concerns.

Is the PTE result valid for all countries?

For how long is the PTE score valid?

The PTE score is valid for two years from the date of the test. After this period, the result will expire, and you will need to retake the test if necessary.
What is the scoring scale for PTE Academic & PTE Core?

The PTE Academic & PTE Core scoring scale ranges from 10 to 90, with scores corresponding to levels on the CEFR (Common European Framework of Reference for Languages). And for PTE Core, it ranges from 10-90 in correspondence with CLB (Canadian Language Benchmark).
How is the PTE Academic & PTE Core test scored?

The test uses an automated AI-based system to evaluate the PTE exam result, ensuring fairness and accuracy. Scores reflect overall English proficiency and individual skills in Speaking, Writing, Reading, and Listening.
What is the minimum score required for admission or visa purposes?

The minimum PTE score depends on the purpose:

- Foundation courses: 36—50
- Undergraduate programs: 51—60
- Postgraduate programs: 57—67

For visa purposes, check specific country and program requirements.
Is there any fee for sending PTE scores?

**No**, sending PTE scores to institutions through your myPTE account is free of charge.
How to check PTE result?

How long does it take for PTE results?

What should I do if I haven't received my PTE result within 48 hours?

What is the difference between the overall score and communicative scores?
