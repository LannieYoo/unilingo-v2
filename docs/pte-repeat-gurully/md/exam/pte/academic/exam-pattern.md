## Understand In-depth PTE Academic Test Format 2026

To succeed in the exam, you must know PTE Academic format well. The exam is computer-based, meaning you'll use a computer, and your responses are scored by advanced algorithms. By understanding the structure, question types, and time limits, you'll manage your time better and perform at your best.

The PTE Academic exam is divided into three main sections: Speaking & Writing, Reading, and Listening. Each section is designed to assess a specific set of language skills; from expressing yourself clearly to comprehending and interpreting spoken or written content.

The exam is fully computer-based, which means you'll be taking it on a computer, and your responses will be instantly recorded and assessed.

## Speaking & Writing

This 76 to 84 minute section, the longest in the PTE Academic format, includes seven question types. It tests speaking and writing skills.

### Personal Introduction

The Personal Introduction helps you get used to the PTE pattern & test technology and prepares you for the speaking and listening questions. You will have 25 seconds to read the prompt and get ready, then 30 seconds to record your answer. You can only record your response once.

**Remember, you are not scored for this part. **

### Read Aloud

| Task | The text will appear on the screen. Read it out loud. |
| --- | --- |
| Prompt Length | Text up to 60 words |
| Skills Assessed | Speaking |
| Time To Answer | It depends on the question and the length of the text |

**How To Answer This Question:**

For this question type, you have to read a written text aloud.

A countdown will show in the recording status box until the microphone opens. You'll have 30—40 seconds to read and prepare. After you hear a short tone, start speaking right away. Do not speak before the microphone opens, or your voice won't be recorded.

- Speak clearly and don't rush.
- Make sure to finish before the progress bar ends. The word "Recording" will change to "Completed."
- You can only record your answer once.

| Time To Answer | 40 seconds |

You'll only hear the audio once and get one chance to record your response. While listening, you can take notes using the erasable whiteboard provided to help you prepare your answer.

## Reading

This 29—30 minute PTE section includes five question types. One of them, 'Reading and Writing: Fill in the Blanks,' assesses both reading & writing skills in an integrated approach.

### Fill in the Blanks (Dropdown):

| Task | Fill the gaps in the text by choosing words from the dropdown menu. |
| --- | --- |
| Prompt Length | Text up to 350 words |
| Skills Assessed | Reading |
| Time To Answer | Not Applicable |

**How To Answer The Question:**

For this question type, you have to choose the correct words from a drop-down list to complete the text.

The passage has missing words, and each gap has a button with a drop-down list. Click the button to view the options and select the one that best fits the gap.

If you change your mind, simply click on a different option.

**Question Is Scored On: Scoring is based on correct/incorrect basis.**

**How To Answer The Question:**

In this question type of PTE exam format, read the passage and choose all the correct answers from the available options.

Select the options you believe are correct by clicking on them with the left mouse button. If you change your mind, click again to deselect.

For this question type, you need to restore the correct order of the text by selecting and dragging the text boxes.

- Left-click on a box to select it, then use the left and right arrow buttons to move it. You can also use the up and down arrows in the right panel to reorder the boxes.

To deselect a box, simply click anywhere else on the screen.

## Listening

The PTE listening section lasts 30—43 minutes and has eight question types based on audio or video clips. Each clip plays once, and you are allowed to take notes.

### Summarise Spoken Text:

| Task | After listening to a recording, write a 50—70 word summary. |
| --- | --- |
| Prompt Length | 60-90 Seconds |
| Skills Assessed | Listening & Writing |
| Time To Answer | Not Applicable |

**How To Answer The Question:**

For this question type, you will listen to an audio recording and then write a 50—70 word summary of what you heard.

You have 10 minutes to listen and write your summary.

The audio will play automatically, and you can listen to it only once.

The Word Count at the bottom will show the number of words you've written. Ensure your summary is at least 50 words but no more than 70 words.

You can use the cut, copy, and paste buttons while writing your summary in this PTE exam format:

- **Cut:** Select the text you want to remove, then click “Cut.”
- **Copy:** Select the text you want to copy, then click “Copy.”
- **Paste:** Place the cursor where you want to paste the text and click “Paste.”

How is the PTE Academic test structured?

The PTE Academic exam follows a comprehensive PTE exam format with three main sections: Speaking and Writing, Reading, and Listening. How is the Speaking and Writing section structured?

The Speaking and Writing section of the pte exam pattern lasts 54 to 67 minutes and consists of seven types of questions. It is the longest section and tests both speaking and writing skills using content that is commonly found in academic settings like school or college.
Is the PTE Academic exam entirely computer-based?

Yes, the PTE Academic exam is entirely computer-based. All tasks are completed on a computer, including speaking, listening, reading, and writing. This ensures that the results are consistent and objective, using automated scoring methods.

The PTE exam pattern reflects this by integrating all components into a digital format, which streamlines the testing process and ensures reliability in scoring.

Are there penalties for incorrect answers in the PTE Academic format?

In the PTE exam, some question types, such as "Highlight Incorrect Words" and "Multiple Choice - Multiple Answer" in the Reading section, have negative marking. No, once you have moved past a question or section in the PTE Academic exam, you cannot go back to change your answers.
