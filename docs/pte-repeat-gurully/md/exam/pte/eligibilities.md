## Valid ID Requirements

When you go to take your test, you must bring a valid physical ID.

- **Your passport is usually required.** If you don't have one, you can use another ID, depending on your PTE test.
- The ID must be valid (not expired), issued within the last 10 years, and match the information you used to book the test.

##### Important Details:

- **Use the same ID you selected when booking the test.** The name, date of birth, citizenship, and gender must exactly match your ID.
- **Bring the original ID.** Photocopies or digital versions are not accepted.
- **Your ID must have a clear and current photo. ** You may be denied entry and lose your test fee if it doesn't match your appearance.

If you're missing your passport due to a visa application, request it back for your test day.

Failure to meet these requirements will result in you not being allowed to take the test and losing your fee.

Check the [exceptions and variations list](https://www.pearsonpte.com/ctf-assets/yqwtwibiobs4/2cxdkBHY1VZppLZ5znzat5/8e54570741052a1cfa2a694c6a0e398b/pte-academic-id-requirements-july-24.pdf) to determine whether you may use an alternative form of ID instead of a passport based on your testing location and citizenship.

## Top PTE Eligible Countries & Score Requirement For Universities

PTE scores vary by country for university admissions and visas.

### Understanding PTE Score Requirements

The Pearson Test of English (PTE) is among the most widely recognized English proficiency tests globally. It is accepted by thousands of universities, colleges, and government agencies, making it a popular choice for academic admissions, visa applications, and immigration processes.

If you're planning to take the PTE, it's crucial to understand the score requirements for different countries and institutions. These requirements vary based on your academic level, the university you're applying to, and the specific visa conditions of each country. Below is an overview of PTE score expectations for some of the top destinations:

### Australia

- **Undergraduate Programs:** Scores between **50 and 65**
- **Postgraduate Programs:** Scores between **60 and 79**
- **Top Universities:** University of Melbourne, Australian National University (ANU), University of Sydney
- **Visa Requirements:** A minimum score of **50** is required for most visa categories.

### United States

- **Undergraduate Programs:** Scores between **50 and 60**
- **Postgraduate Programs:** Scores between **55 and 70**
- **Top Universities:** Harvard University, Stanford University, Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)
- **Visa Requirements:** PTE scores for an F-1 student visa depend on the university's requirements.

### United Kingdom

- **Undergraduate Programs:** Scores between **51 and 60**
- **Postgraduate Programs:** Scores between **59 and 70**
- **Top Universities:** University of Oxford, University of Cambridge, London School of Economics (LSE)
- **Visa Requirements:** Must meet the specific requirements of the university and the UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI).

## FAQs

Got questions about the PTE exam eligibility? Explore our detailed FAQs for answers to all your queries.
Is PTE Academic valid for Australian PR?

**Yes**, PTE Academic is widely accepted for Australian Permanent Residency (PR) applications. The Australian government recognizes it for immigration purposes.
Is PTE accepted in Canada?

**Yes**, PTE Academic is accepted in Canada for study purposes by numerous Canadian universities and colleges. However, for immigration (PR) purposes, PTE Core is now accepted by IRCC (Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada).
Is PTE accepted in Germany?

**Yes**, PTE Academic is accepted by several universities and institutions in Germany as proof of English language proficiency for admission to academic programs.
What are the accepted forms of ID for the PTE exam?

A valid passport is the primary form of ID required for the test. Alternative IDs may be accepted based on your test location and citizenship, as per the exceptions and variations list.
What is the minimum age requirement to appear for the PTE exam?

The PTE exam eligibility criteria require candidates to be at least 16 years old. Minors (16 or 17 years old) must provide parental or guardian consent.
Do I need to be a citizen of a specific country to take the PTE exam?

**No**, the **PTE exam eligibility criteria** do not impose any citizenship or residency restrictions. Candidates from all over the world are eligible to take the test.
Is there a minimum educational qualification required for the PTE exam?

**No**, there are no minimum educational qualifications required. The **PTE exam eligibility** allows candidates from any educational background to appear for the test.
Is PTE accepted in the USA?

**Yes**, numerous universities and colleges accept PTE Academic in the USA for undergraduate and postgraduate admissions.
Is PTE accepted in the UK?

**Yes**, PTE Academic is accepted in the United Kingdom for student visas, work visas, and university admissions. The UK Home Office approves it for various visa categories, including the Skilled Worker Visa and the Student Route.
Is PTE accepted in New Zealand?

**Yes**, PTE Academic is widely accepted in New Zealand for immigration purposes, including student visas, skilled migration, and residency applications. It is recognized by the Immigration New Zealand (INZ) and many educational institutions.
Which countries accept PTE Academic for immigration?

PTE Academic is accepted for immigration purposes in countries like Australia, Canada, New Zealand, Singapore, the UK, the USA, Ireland, and Germany. It is widely recognized as proof of English proficiency for visa applications and permanent residency in these countries.
Are there any exceptions to the ID requirements for PTE candidates?

**Yes**, some locations allow alternative forms of ID based on the candidate's citizenship and local regulations. For details, refer to the **PTE exam eligibility criteria**.
Can students with special needs or disabilities take the PTE exam?

**Yes**, PTE Academic accommodates students with special needs or disabilities by providing tailored arrangements such as extended time or separate testing rooms.
What are the PTE score requirements for admission to top universities?

The required scores vary:

- **Australia:** 50-79
- **USA:** 50-70
- **UK:** 51-70
- **Canada:** 55-75
- **New Zealand:** 50-75
