PTE Speaking Practice Questions

Understand speaking section question types.

Read Aloud

In this task, you'll be asked to read the provided text out loud. Before the recording begins, a countdown will appear in the status box, giving you about 30—40 seconds to read and get ready.

Practice Know more
Prompt Length Skills Assessed No. of Question
Text up to 60 words Speaking (PTE Academic), Reading & Speaking (PTE Core) 6-7

Repeat Sentence

In the PTE Repeat Sentence task, you need to carefully listen to the sentence and repeat it clearly at a natural pace. The audio starts on its own, and as soon as it ends, the microphone activates with a “Recording” prompt—begin speaking right away.

Practice Know more
Prompt Length Skills Assessed No. of Question
3-9 Seconds Listening & Speaking 10-12

Describe Image

In this task, you'll need to describe the image displayed in the question. You'll get 25 seconds to prepare, and once the microphone starts recording, you should begin speaking immediately.

Practice Know more
Prompt Length Skills Assessed No. of Question
Not applicable Speaking 5-6 (PTE Academic), 3-4 (PTE Core)

Re-tell Lecture (only in PTE Academic)

In this task, you need to summarize what you heard in the audio. It will play automatically, and there might be an accompanying image. After the audio ends, you'll have 10 seconds to get ready before responding.

Practice Know more
Prompt Length Skills Assessed No. of Question
Upto 90 Seconds Listening & Speaking 2-3

Respond to a Situation (PTE Core & PTE Academic)

In this task, you'll hear and read about a situation. After the audio finishes, in PTE Core you will have 20 seconds to prepare and 40 seconds to speak, while in PTE Academic you will have 10 seconds to prepare and 40 seconds to speak. Be sure to provide a well-detailed response.

Practice Know more
Prompt Length Skills Assessed No. of Question
Text up to 60 words Speaking 2-3 (PTE Academic), 2- 4 ( PTE Core)

Summarize Group Discussion

In the PTE Summarize Group Discussion task, you'll hear a conversation among three people and you need to summarize their discussion in your own words within two minutes.

Practice Know more
Prompt Length Skills Assessed No. of Question
Up to 3 Minutes Listening & Speaking 2-3

Answer Short Question

In this task, you'll hear a question and need to respond briefly, typically with just one or a few words. The audio plays automatically, and an image may appear to help you understand the question.

Practice Know more
Prompt Length Skills Assessed No. of Question
3-9 Seconds Listening (PTE Academic), Listening & Speaking (PTE Core) 5-6

PTE Writing Practice Questions

Understand writing section question types.

Summarize Written Text

In this task, you'll be given a passage—up to 300 words for PTE Academic and up to 200 words for PTE Core—and you need to write a summary. For PTE Academic, condense it into one sentence of 5—75 words, and for PTE Core, into 1—2 sentences of 25—50 words, all within 10 minutes.

Practice Know more
Prompt Length Skills Assessed No. of Question
Text up to 300 words (PTE Academic) & 200 words(PTE Core) Reading & Writing 2 (PTE Academic), 1-2 (PTE Core)

Write Essay (PTE Academic)

In this task, you need to write a 200—300 word argumentative essay responding to the given prompt, and you'll have 20 minutes to finish it.

Practice Know more
Prompt Length Skills Assessed No. of Question
2-3 Sentences Writing 1

Write Email (PTE Core)

In this task, you'll need to write an email based on a given prompt, usually describing a situation or request. Your response should be 80—120 words long, and you'll have 9 minutes to complete it.

Practice Know more
Prompt Length Skills Assessed No. of Question
Text up to 100 words Writing 2-3

How to Take the PTE Practice Test

Follow these key steps while taking a PTE practice test to stay aligned with exam requirements.

  1. 1

    Understand the Test Structure Clearly

    First thing you need to do is familiarise yourself with the PTE exam format. PTE exam is divided into four sections- Speaking, Writing, Reading & Listening. Every section has a different task which evaluates English speaking, writing, reading & listening abilities. So, when you become familiar with the task through PTE practice online, you can plan your responses better and reduce stress during the real test.

  2. 2

    Select a Reliable Practice Platform

    The second thing you need to do is select the best PTE practice test platform that mimics the real exam, such as Gurully. Opt for platforms that provide AI-based scoring and detailed reports, so you can trust the accuracy of your results and track your progress effectively.

  3. 3

    Practice in Real Exam Conditions

    Dedicate a quiet space where you won't be disturbed. Wear headphones, keep the timer on, and avoid pausing between tasks. Training yourself under the same pressure as the actual exam builds mental stamina, enhances focus, and prepares you for the three-hour challenge ahead.

  4. 4

    Attempt Full-Length PTE Practice Test Regularly

    Along with doing individual task practice for the PTE exam focus on focus on full-length mock test. Take a full-length PTE practice test online to understand how your performance changes over time. This also helps you learn how to conserve energy, maintain concentration, and manage stress across all four modules of the exam.

  5. 5

    Review Every Score Report in Detail

    After finishing a PTE exam practice test, go through your performance breakdown carefully. Identify weak areas such as pronunciation, grammar, vocabulary, spelling, or listening comprehension. Write these down and build a targeted improvement plan instead of practicing randomly.

  6. 6

    Focused Practice on Weak Areas

    Once you know your mistakes, dedicate extra time to those specific skills. For example, if you find listening difficult, practice dictations and summarize spoken text exercises daily. If speaking is your weak point, work on Read Aloud and Repeat Sentence for 15 minutes every day. Focused effort leads to faster improvement.

Tips to Achieve Your Target Score

Practical strategies and focused preparation methods to help you reach your desired PTE score with confidence.

Set Section-Wise Goals

Break your target into smaller goals such as aiming for 65 in Speaking, 70 in Reading, and so on. Achieving smaller milestones motivates you and keeps preparation structured.

Stay Consistent with Practice

Even short daily sessions are powerful. Practicing for 40—45 minutes each day builds retention and fluency better than occasional long study hours.

Improve Time Management

Every second matters in PTE. Train yourself to move on when stuck. Spending too long on one question can cost you marks in later sections.

Work on Pronunciation and Fluency

Speaking does not require a native accent. Focus on clear pronunciation, steady pace, and smooth delivery without unnecessary pauses.

Read High-Quality Sample Answers

Compare your answers with high-scoring samples. Notice sentence structure, vocabulary, and organization, then apply the same techniques in your practice.

Expand Vocabulary Naturally

Read newspapers, academic articles, or listen to podcasts daily. Exposure to diverse vocabulary improves performance in both Reading and Writing tasks.

PTE Study Plan For 2026

Smart and effective PTE Study Plan for 2026 to help you prepare strategically and achieve your desired score.

Week 1: Build Strong Foundations

Objective: Understand the exam, learn strategies, and start section-wise drills.

  • Get familiar with the test format: While starting practice for PTE exam, spend time knowing how each task contributes to your score. This avoids wasting effort on low-weightage tasks. You can also take one PTE practice test free on Gurully.
  • Speaking: Begin with Read Aloud and Repeat Sentence. Record yourself daily and compare to improve fluency.
  • Writing: Learn the format for Summarize Written Text and Essay Writing. Start small—practice one task a day.
  • Reading: Focus on Fill in the Blanks. This helps sharpen vocabulary and grammar in context.
  • Listening: Warm up by listening to short audios or podcasts. Write down key words—this builds your base for Write from Dictation.

Practice tip: Take Gurully's section-wise practice tests in Week 1 itself. They'll help you identify strengths and weaknesses early, especially PTE Academic Practice Test.

Week 2: Skill Building & Accuracy

Objective: Strengthen techniques and focus on accuracy.

  • Speaking Add Describe Image and Re-tell Lecture. Focus on smooth delivery instead of memorizing words.
  • Writing: Write one essay every alternate day. For summaries, practice writing concise one-line answers.
  • Reading: Work on Re-order Paragraphs and Multiple Choice. They improve your logical flow and reading comprehension.
  • Listening: Start with Summarize Spoken Text. Listen actively, note keywords, and form short, clear summaries.

Practice tip: Take section section-wise PTE practice test on Gurully to test accuracy. Don't just look at your score—review where you lost marks and adjust.

Week 3: Test-Like Practice

Objective: Practice under real exam conditions.

  • Attempt at least 2 full-length PTE exam practice test this week. Treat them like the real exam—no pausing, no breaks.
  • Revisit feedback from your practice tests. Fix repeated errors, especially in high-weightage tasks like Repeat Sentence, Write from Dictation, and Summarize Written Text.
  • Balance your schedule: Mix full-length PTE practice tests with section-wise practice so you can zoom in on problem areas.

Pro Tip: Gurully's AI-driven scoring gives you a detailed breakdown—use it to understand not just what went wrong but also why.

Week 4: Final Polishing & Confidence Boost

Objective: Revise, sharpen, and prepare for the big day.

  • Speaking: Do short daily drills—Repeat Sentence + Read Aloud. These tasks carry heavy weight.
  • Writing: Revise templates and focus on completing essays in 20 minutes. Keep them clear and structured.
  • Reading & Listening: Focus on accuracy. A wrong answer can lower your score more than skipping.
  • Attempt 2—3 more full-length PTE mock tests this week to build exam stamina.
  • Last 3 days: Stop learning new things. Instead, revise notes, review mistakes, and stay calm.

Final Prep Tip: Mix full mocks with quick section-wise practice tests. For example, do a 20-minute Listening practice one day and a Reading mini-test the next. Gurully's platform makes it easy to customize your prep this way.

Daily Study Time Split

Beginners: 3 hours/day → 1 hr Speaking, 1 hr Writing, 30 mins Reading, 30 mins Listening.

Second-time test takers: 2 hours/day → 1 hr on your weakest module, 30 mins high-weightage tasks, 30 mins mock practice.
